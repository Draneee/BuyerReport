Hair Styling Irons Buyer's Guide

Samuel Gilmour

With natural hair, every morning is a surprise hairdo. Are you going to have curls that look like they've been struck by electricity? Are you going to have a mysterious, creased wave that looks like pleated pants? While we're grateful for those natural waves, curls, or straight strands, it's also nice to know exactly how your hair will be looking for the day.

Hair styling irons have come a long way in the fashion industry. Lucky for us, we don't have to get the clothes iron out just for that straight-hair look. We can forget about those insane curling techniques too, which are both expensive and simply ridiculous.
Now we have portable, easy-to-use hair styling irons that can be used anywhere at almost any time. Whether you're an on-the-go professional or just want to spend the day splurging on yourself, hair styling irons are the tools you can easily add to your beauty toolkit.

Curling Irons

Picking out a curling iron that's right for your hair should be fun and simple. After all, you're choosing the exact curl that will make your hair stand out the way you want it to.

When looking for a new curling iron, you'll find out that each one is a certain barrel size, a different heating material, and a different shape. Your choice will depend on the specific look you're going for, which can be exciting and daunting all at the same ime

Since curling irons are distributing heat to your hair, you want to make sure they're coated in a material that gets the job done. For fine hair, ceramic is an inexpensive, easy-to-find option. Since it doesn't reach intolerable temperatures, it won't commit heat crimes against your hair. Titanium barrels can get paired up with thicker hair since it gets consistently hot. For the fancy, pinkies-up, thick hair folks, you can get a gold-plated curling iron that lasts longer and distributes a high amount of heat while in use.
Tourmaline is a common material used in curling irons, and you might see it as a hybrid with ceramic to make it suitable for more hair types than just thick hair. Alone, tourmaline works best for thicker hair thanks to its infrared technology that counteracts frizz.
